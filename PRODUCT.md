# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary visitor is a Madison-area parent who arrived via referral. A friend, neighbor, or family member said "you should book Cassie for newborn / family / first-birthday photos," and the visitor is now checking the site to confirm that recommendation. They are not comparison-shopping. They are looking for permission to trust the choice they have already half-made.

Secondary visitors include local Google searchers (Madison and Waunakee plus "photographer") who are earlier in the funnel and need both proof of quality and a sense of Cassie as a person before they reach out. A smaller share are existing clients returning to share their gallery with family.

The job to be done: "Show me this is the real person my friend talked about, and that her work matches the moments I want to remember."

## Product Purpose

The site is the credibility layer in a word-of-mouth business. It does not need to convert cold traffic. It needs to validate warm referrals quickly and convincingly. Success is when a referred visitor lands, browses for two minutes, feels what their friend felt, and reaches out without ever questioning whether Cassie is the right photographer. Failure is when the site introduces hesitation that the referral had already removed.

## Brand Personality

Bright, airy, joyful. Natural light over studio strobe. Real laughter over posed grins. The voice is warm without being saccharine, confident without being clinical. Cassie should read as a friend a Madison family would happily invite into their living room at 6 a.m. on a Saturday for a newborn session, not as a service provider behind a logo.

Emotional target: a parent should feel calmer scrolling than they did before they arrived. The implicit promise is "your kids will be comfortable with me, and the photos will look like the day you actually lived."

## Anti-references

Two failure modes to actively avoid:

1. **Squarespace-template photographer.** Off-the-shelf themes (Brine, Foster, Wells, and their imitators) have saturated the photographer market. A serif logo over a hero photo, a centered three-column services grid, a black-or-white-on-cream palette, and an Instagram embed at the bottom. The template signals "I picked this in an afternoon," which undermines the trust the referral built.

2. **Bargain or chain studio.** Walmart Portrait Studio, Olan Mills, JCPenney mall photographer energy. Heavy retouching, fake backdrops, stiff posing, watermarked thumbnails, "package pricing" tables. Anything that reads as commodity. Cassie's clients are paying for a personal artist, not a coupon transaction.

Beyond those two, also avoid: hero video loops, parallax-fade hero copy, scripty wedding-blog typography in every section, gradient text effects, watermarks burned into thumbnails, and stock-photo placeholder vibes.

## Design Principles

1. **The photos are the argument.** Copy describes services; photographs prove them. Resist filling negative space with adjectives. When in doubt, fewer words and bigger images.

2. **Trust is built quietly.** Visitors arrive warm. The site should never overcorrect into hustle. No aggressive CTAs, no "limited spots," no pop-ups. One clear inquiry path, placed where it is genuinely needed.

3. **Bright, not moody.** Tonal choices must support the family-newborn personality. Editorial moodiness, deep shadows, and high-contrast drama belong to a different photographer's brand. Cassie's site reads as morning light.

4. **Local, not generic.** Madison and Waunakee are real places with real weather, real seasons, and a real community. The site can look like Wisconsin without becoming kitsch. It must never look like it could be any photographer in any city.

5. **Cassie is a real human, not a logo.** Her face, her voice, and her words appear on the page. The About section reads like someone introducing herself, not like SEO copy. This is the strongest counter to template fatigue and chain-studio anonymity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: WCAG 2.1 AA throughout. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for the hero slider, lightbox transitions, and any scroll-driven animation; users who set the OS preference receive static images and instant transitions. Maintain visible focus states on every interactive element. Provide meaningful `alt` text on portfolio images (subject and session type, not "image") and clear form labels with inline validation.
